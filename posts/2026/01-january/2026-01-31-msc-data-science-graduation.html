This one's a bit different from the usual technical posts. On 22 January 2026, I officially graduated with a Master of Science in Data Science from the University of Wolverhampton.

The journey

Completing an MSc while working full-time was one of the hardest things I've done. There were late nights, weekends spent buried in research papers, and more than a few moments where I questioned whether I could get through it all. But every module, every assignment, and every challenge taught me something I now use in my day-to-day work.

The programme covered everything from statistical modelling and machine learning to natural language processing and big data technologies. It gave me the theoretical foundations to complement the practical skills I'd been building on the job.

What's next

I am excited to apply the skills I have learned during my MSc at University Hospitals Birmingham NHS Foundation Trust, supporting the NHS England 10 Year Plan's focus on digitisation and patient empowerment.

Through advanced analytics, we will uncover hidden trends in our patient feedback data, driving continual improvement and ensuring we consistently deliver excellent, compassionate care to the millions we serve. Some of this work is already underway - I'm building an HCAT complaint classification model using DeBERTa, and developing a triangulation project that brings together patient experience and patient relations data using NMF topic modelling techniques to surface patterns across feedback sources.

This blog has been part of that journey too - a place to document what I'm building and learning. There's plenty more to come.
